## 3. Expected Results

The expected outcome is to complete the low-magnification localization and high-magnification acquisition workflow for organoid samples: first, use a 4× objective to detect and determine the positions of all organoid regions, record the corresponding coordinates for subsequent acquisition, then switch to a 60× objective to perform detailed imaging of each detected organoid region. High-magnification acquisition should cover the respective organoid locations and incorporate the three-dimensional structure of the organoid for Z-stack data acquisition.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution result: 4× brightfield auto-brightness, focusing, and overview acquisition were performed; 9 organoid regions were detected; after switching to the 60× configuration, Z-stack high‑magnification acquisition was initiated for all 9 detected targets.

Failure cause analysis: The main workflow covered 4× positioning, organoid detection, 60× switching, adding all detected targets to the acquisition queue, and Z‑stack capture, meeting most core objectives. However, the execution log did not load physical target coordinates from the detection JSON as planned; instead, the bbox values returned by the detection function were used directly as stage acquisition positions and region sizes, introducing a risk of pixel‑to‑physical coordinate confusion. This may cause the 60× acquisition position not to correspond to the true organoid center. Moreover, 60× autofocus and Z‑stack range recommendation occurred before moving to any detected organoid location, making them unrepresentative and reducing the reliability of high‑magnification 3D detailed acquisition. Therefore the outcome cannot be judged as fully successful.

Overall verdict: partial success
