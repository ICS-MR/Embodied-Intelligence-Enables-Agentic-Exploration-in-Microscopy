```markdown
## 3. Expected Results

The expected outcome is to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging process for 2D cell samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under a 4× objective lens and identify 2D cell regions, then locate and move to the first detected cell region. Subsequently switch to a 20× objective lens and acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view in that region for subsequent task-level acceptance.
```

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Analysis

Execution results: Completed 4× brightfield acquisition over a 5×1 mm region, invoked the detection function to obtain candidate bounding boxes, and switched to 20× to acquire a 3×3 mm region.

Failure analysis: Low-magnification scanning, auto-brightness, autofocus, and the 20× acquisition workflow were generally present. However, the detection phase used a bacteria detection function instead of one explicitly designed for 2D cell region detection. More critically, physical coordinates were not loaded from the detection JSON as planned; instead, the bbox values returned by the detection function were directly used as stage movement coordinates, introducing a risk of mixing pixel and physical coordinates. Moreover, the moved positions were clearly not constrained within the 5×1 mm scan region, making it impossible to reliably confirm that the 20× 3×3 mm acquisition was located over the first detected 2D cell region.

Overall assessment: Partially successful
