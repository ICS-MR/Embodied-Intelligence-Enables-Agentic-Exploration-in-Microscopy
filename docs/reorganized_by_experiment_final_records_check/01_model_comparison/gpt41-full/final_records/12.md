## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The workflow completed 4× brightfield acquisition of a 5×1 mm area and organoid detection, then switched to 20× to acquire a 3×3 mm brightfield area, but the high-magnification acquisition was still performed at the original position.

Failure cause analysis: The critical step “Move to the first detected area” did not actually execute – the task manager returned an error at that step. Therefore, it cannot be confirmed that the 20× 3×3 mm acquisition was located at the first detected organoid region. Furthermore, organoids are three-dimensional targets, and the final 20× acquisition explicitly lacked a Z-stack configuration, with no explanation provided for single-plane acquisition, posing a risk of insufficient three-dimensional imaging information. The low-magnification scanning and detection portions largely met the requirements.

Comprehensive judgment: Partially successful
