## 3. Expected Results

Bright-field/transmitted light and multiple fluorescence channel imaging of the organoid sample should be completed, including Z-stack acquisition covering the organoid thickness range. After projection, each fluorescence signal should be overlaid with the bright-field channel (Gray/Grey/brightfield) using merge_channels(). The output should reflect the organoid structure and the spatial correspondence of different fluorescence signals against the transmitted light background.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: Brightfield, DAPI, FITC, and TRITC single-layer images of the organoid at the current position were acquired, and a merged fluorescence channel image was generated, but the final overlay of fluorescence and transmitted light was not successfully saved.

Failure cause analysis: The task objective required brightfield and multi-fluorescence signal acquisition of the organoid and overlay with transmitted light; the records indeed show acquisition of brightfield and multiple fluorescence channels, and the fluorescence exposure, transmitted light shutoff, and autofocus procedures were generally reasonable. However, organoids are three-dimensional samples, and the final acquisition stage clearly did not configure Z-stack, nor was projection performed before merging, which does not meet the task-specific success criteria. In the image analysis stage, only the fluorescence channels were merged first, then brightfield was overlaid manually using addWeighted, and an error occurred when constructing ImageWithMetadata due to an unwhitelisted call, resulting in the final composite_fluorescence_brightfield.tif not being saved. Therefore, the core acquisition part was completed, but there were critical deficiencies in 3D acquisition/projection and the final overlay output.

Overall assessment: Partial success
