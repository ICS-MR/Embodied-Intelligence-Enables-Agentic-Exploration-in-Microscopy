## 2. Biological Samples Used

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The workflow switched to 4× brightfield and acquired a 5×1 mm area, performed organoid detection and moved to the first detection region, then switched to 20× brightfield and acquired a 3×3 mm field of view.

Failure Cause Analysis: The core steps of low-magnification scanning, detection, moving to the first target, and 20× large field-of-view acquisition were all executed, and using the target coordinates based on the detection result center is reasonable; however, organoids are three-dimensional structures, and the final 20× target acquisition stage was explicitly not configured with Z-stack, nor was a reasonable explanation provided for acquiring only a single layer. Therefore, the requirements for three-dimensional target imaging are not fully met.

Overall Judgment: Partial Success
