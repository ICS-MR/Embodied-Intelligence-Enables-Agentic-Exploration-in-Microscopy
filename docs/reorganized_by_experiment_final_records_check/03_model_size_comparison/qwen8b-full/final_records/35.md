# Experiment Record 35

## 1. User Input

```text
Imaging target: Organoids; use a low-magnification objective to collect the positions of organoids in a 1 mm circular gel droplet, then sequentially collect the status of organoids using a 20× objective.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to achieve low-magnification, full-area localization and acquisition of organoids within a 1 mm circular gel droplet. A ~1000 × 1000 µm square field of view will cover the region and identify the coordinates of each organoid. The system will then switch to a 20× objective to sequentially acquire a status image of each organoid according to the coordinates, and at the high-magnification stage configure or appropriately consider Z‑stack acquisition to reflect the three‑dimensional structural state of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The workflow completed the acquisition of a ~1000×1000 μm area under 4× brightfield, performed organoid detection, and saved the position JSON. Subsequently, automated acquisition with Z-stack was configured at the detected positions under 20×.

Failure Cause Analysis: The low-magnification coverage of the circular gel droplet, automatic brightness/focus, detection JSON, and the sequential addition of 20× acquisition positions per target generally satisfied the core workflow. However, during the 20× phase, before loading and moving to the first organoid position, autofocus and Z-stack range recommendation were performed at the original low-magnification center field of view. The recommended focal plane and stack range may not be applicable to each target organoid, affecting the reliability of 3D state acquisition. Additionally, the high-magnification channel was arbitrarily switched to DAPI fluorescence, while the task did not specify DAPI labeling, which may not reflect the organoid state. Repeatedly adding the same channel within the loop also caused redundant acquisition. Although the core acquisition chain exists, risks in high-magnification target calibration and imaging mode lead to a judgment of partial success.

Overall Judgment: Partial Success
