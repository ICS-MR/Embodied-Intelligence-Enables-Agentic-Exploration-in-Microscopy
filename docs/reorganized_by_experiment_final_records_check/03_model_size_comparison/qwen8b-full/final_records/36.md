# Experiment Record 36

## 1. User Input

```text
Imaging target: 2D section; acquire brightfield and images of different fluorescent signals, and overlay the fluorescent signals with transmitted light.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

2D section

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected that brightfield or transmitted light images of the two-dimensional slices will be acquired, along with images from multiple different fluorescence signal channels. Each fluorescence channel, after channel separation, should be merged with the brightfield image to form a composite image where fluorescence signals are superimposed on the transmitted light background.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Analysis

Execution results: The acquisition of brightfield, FITC, and TRITC three-channel images at 20× the current field of view was actually completed, and an attempt was made to generate a merged overlay image.

Failure analysis: The acquisition part largely covered the task objectives; the brightfield had autobrightness and autofocus, and the fluorescence channels were switched and multiple signals were acquired. However, in the image analysis stage, split_channels() was only performed on the brightfield image, and the input to merge_channels() did not include the loaded FITC and TRITC images; therefore, the generated overlay image cannot prove that the fluorescence signals were overlaid onto the transmitted light image. In addition, the fluorescence exposure merely used the current 100 ms; although barely acceptable, parameter optimization is insufficient.
