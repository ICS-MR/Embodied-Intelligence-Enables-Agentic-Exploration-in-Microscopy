## 2. Biological Samples Used

2D section

## 3. Expected Results

The 2D slice sample is expected to be scanned in brightfield at 10× over a 4×4 mm area, and based on the scan results, 2D cells are to be detected and the first detection region located. The objective is then switched to 40× in that region, followed by sequential fluorescence imaging in green and red channels over a 2×2 mm field of view, with the corresponding fluorescence intensity measurements output.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Analysis

Execution result: Completed 10× brightfield 4×4 mm scanning, 2D cell detection, movement to the first detection area, sequential acquisition under 40× for FITC/TRITC, and invoked fluorescence intensity quantification.

Failure cause analysis: The main workflow covered most core steps of the task, and auto brightness and auto focus were performed before brightfield acquisition, with detection results also used to move to the first target center; however, during the 40× fluorescence acquisition stage, 2×2 mm was mistakenly converted to 20000×20000 μm, i.e., the actual configuration was 20×20 mm / 2 cm×2 cm, rather than the 2000×2000 μm field of view required by the task. Consequently, the final FITC/TRITC images and their intensity measurements do not correspond to the specified 2×2 mm area, and thus can only be judged as partially successful.

Overall judgment: Partial success
