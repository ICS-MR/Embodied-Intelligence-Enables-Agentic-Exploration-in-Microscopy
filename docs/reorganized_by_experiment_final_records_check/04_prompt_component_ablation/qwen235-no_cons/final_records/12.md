## 2. Biological Samples Used

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The workflow completed 4× brightfield 5×1 mm area scanning, organoid detection, loading and moving to the center of the first detected organoid, and switching to 20× to acquire a 3×3 mm brightfield area.

Failure Cause Analysis: The core low-magnification scanning, detection, moving to the first detection area, and 20× large field-of-view acquisition all have execution records, and automatic brightness and autofocus were performed before brightfield acquisition. However, organoids are three-dimensional targets, and the final 20× target acquisition phase was explicitly not configured with a Z-stack, nor was a reasonable explanation provided for single-plane acquisition. Therefore, the high-magnification imaging cannot fully meet the requirements for three-dimensional organoid acquisition.

Overall Judgment: Partial Success
