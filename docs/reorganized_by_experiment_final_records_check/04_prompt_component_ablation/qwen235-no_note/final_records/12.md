## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Analysis

Execution results: The workflow switched to 4× and acquired a 5×1 mm brightfield area, performed organoid detection and generated JSON, then switched to 20× and acquired a 3×3 mm brightfield area at the current position.

Failure analysis: The record did not load the detected organoid_locations_list.json or use the detection results to move to the first detection area; the 20× acquisition still used the current XY position, so the critical low-to-high magnification target localization workflow was not completed. Additionally, organoids are 3D targets, and the final 20× acquisition explicitly did not configure a Z-stack, nor did it re-autofocus after switching to 20×, further reducing the reliability of the final imaging.

Overall assessment: Failure
