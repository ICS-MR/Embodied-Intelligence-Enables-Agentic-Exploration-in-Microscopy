```text
Imaging target: Organoids; switch to low-magnification brightfield, capture the 2×2 mm array area, locate organoids based on brightfield information, switch to the 20× objective, and sequentially capture the blue, green, and red fluorescent channels of each organoid.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete low-magnification brightfield imaging of a 2×2 mm array region of the organoid sample, and from the brightfield image, obtain and load the coordinates of each organoid via organoid localization analysis. Subsequently, switch to a 20× objective, acquire images at each organoid position in the order of blue, green, and red fluorescence channels, and configure or cover the necessary Z-axis range according to organoid thickness.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The 4x brightfield 2×2 mm area acquisition was completed, and organoid positions were detected and saved; however, the 20x blue, green, and red fluorescence Z-stack acquisition threw an error due to a conflict between the Z-stack start/end direction and step size, and the final acquisition was not performed.

Failure cause analysis: The low-magnification brightfield acquisition included automatic brightness and focusing, and the area size and positioning steps largely met requirements; but during the high-magnification stage, autofocus was executed while still in brightfield mode with brightness set to 0, posing a critical risk to focus reliability. Subsequently, the range returned by `z_stack_range()` was passed to `set_z_stack` in the wrong order, causing the execution error “Z-axis step direction conflicts with start and end range” and leaving `run_acquisition` incomplete. Therefore, no 20x blue/green/red fluorescence images were obtained for each organoid, and the core objective was not achieved.

Overall assessment: failure
