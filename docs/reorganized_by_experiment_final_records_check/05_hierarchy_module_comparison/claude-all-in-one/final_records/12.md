## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution result: The workflow first switched to 4× and performed brightfield brightness adjustment and focusing, but then switched to 10× to capture a 5×1 mm area, did not perform organoid detection and did not move to the first detection area, and finally switched to 20× at the current position to capture a 3×3 mm brightfield image.

Failure cause analysis: The core task required scanning a 5×1 mm area at 4×, detecting organoids, moving to the first detection area, and then capturing a 3×3 mm field at 20×; in the record, the scanning phase switched to 10× instead of maintaining 4×, did not call any organoid detection function or generate/load detection positions, and the move step only reported an error indicating no detection and did not actually move, so the 20× acquisition was not targeting the first detected organoid area. In addition, organoids are 3D targets, and the final 20× acquisition was not configured with or did not reasonably consider Z-stack. Key detection, positioning, and targeted high-magnification acquisition were all not completed.

Comprehensive judgment: Failure
