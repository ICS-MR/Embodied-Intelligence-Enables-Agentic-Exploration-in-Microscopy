## 2. Biological Samples Used

2D section

## 3. Expected Results

Expected to achieve a low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for 2D slice samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under a 4× objective and complete 2D cell region detection, then locate and move to the first detected cell region, and acquire a high-magnification image covering a 3×3 mm field of view under a 20× objective.

## 4. Execution Result and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution result: The workflow first switched to 4× and completed basic brightfield settings, but the subsequent 5×1 mm scan was changed to 40× acquisition; 2D cell region detection was not performed, yet an attempt was made to load targets from the never-generated target_locations.json and move the stage, and finally, at that unreliable position, switched to 20× to acquire a 3×3 mm region.

Failure cause analysis: The core task requirement was to scan a 5×1 mm region at 4×, detect 2D cell regions, and then move to the first detected region for 20× acquisition of a 3×3 mm area. In the recorded process, the scanning stage erroneously switched to 40× instead of staying at 4×, performed acquisition only without invoking any 2D cell detection/target localization analysis, and did not generate or save the corresponding detection result file. The subsequent loading of target_locations.json did not match the preceding output, and moving to "the first detected region" lacked valid evidence; thus the final 20× acquisition cannot be proven to cover the first detected cell region. Although some hardware settings and the final acquisition action existed, the critical link from low-magnification detection to high-magnification localization was not completed.

Overall assessment: Failure
