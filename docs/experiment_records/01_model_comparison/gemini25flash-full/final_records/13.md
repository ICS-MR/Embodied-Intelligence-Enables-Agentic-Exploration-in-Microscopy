# Experiment Record 12

## 1. User Input

```text
[input 1] Imaging target: Organoids; switch to the 4× objective
[input 2] scan a 5×1 mm region to detect organoids
[input 3] move to the first detected region
[input 4] switch to the 20× objective, and acquire images of a 3×3 mm field of view.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The system recorded a 4× brightfield 5×1 mm area Z-stack scan and organoid detection, and acquired a 3×3 mm brightfield area at 20×, but did not execute the move to the first detected region.

Failure analysis: The core workflow lacked the “move to the first detected region” step: after detection, the task manager returned NO and did not load the detection JSON or move the stage; the subsequent 20× acquisition still used the current XY position rather than the center of the first detected organoid, thus failing to guarantee capturing the target organoid. Additionally, organoids are 3D targets, and the final 20× acquisition was explicitly not configured with Z-stack, which also does not meet the requirements for high-magnification acquisition of 3D targets.

Overall assessment: Failure
