## 1. User Input

```text
[input 2] scan a 5×1 mm region to detect organoids
[input 3] move to the first detected region
[input 4] switch to the 20× objective, and acquire images of a 3×3 mm field of view.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete the low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for organoid samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under the 4× objective and perform organoid detection, then locate and move to the first detected region. Finally, acquire a high-magnification image of a 3×3 mm field of view for that region under the 20× objective, and configure or reasonably consider Z-stack acquisition based on the three-dimensional characteristics of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The workflow completed acquisition of a 4× brightfield 5×1 mm region and also acquired a 3×3 mm brightfield region at 20×, but did not successfully output organoid detection results, nor moved to the first detection region.
Failure analysis: The image analysis step threw an error due to use of a prohibited `open` operation; there were no reliable organoid detection results or JSON output usable for subsequent localization in the records. Subsequently, the 20× acquisition moved directly to an unfounded fixed coordinate (1500,1500) rather than the center of the first detected organoid, so the core closed-loop from low-mag detection to high-mag target acquisition failed. Additionally, because organoids are 3D targets, the final 20× acquisition did not configure or account for Z-stack, further failing to meet task-specific requirements.

Overall assessment: Failure
