## 2. Biological Samples Used

2D section

## 3. Expected Results

Expected to achieve a low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for 2D slice samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under a 4× objective and complete 2D cell region detection, then locate and move to the first detected cell region, and acquire a high-magnification image covering a 3×3 mm field of view under a 20× objective.

## 4. Execution Result and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution result: The workflow switched to 4× brightfield and acquired a 5×1 mm area, then switched to 20× and acquired a 3×3 mm brightfield area; however, the final high‑magnification acquisition was performed at the original/fixed coordinates rather than at the first detected 2D cell region.
Failure analysis: The low‑magnification scanning and acquisition were largely completed, but the 2D cell detection step included a prohibited “open” file operation and reported an error, and no reliable detection result appears in the records. Subsequently, the position of the first detected region was neither loaded nor used; instead the system moved directly to the fixed current coordinates for the 20× acquisition. Therefore the key step of “move to the first detected region” was missing, and the final high‑magnification image cannot be guaranteed to cover the target area. This task was a 2D section; a Z‑stack as required for 3D samples is not needed.

Overall assessment: Partial success
