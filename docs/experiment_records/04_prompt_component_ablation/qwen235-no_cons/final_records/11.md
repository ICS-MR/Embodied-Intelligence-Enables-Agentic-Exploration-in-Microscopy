## 1. User Input

```text
[input 1] Imaging target: 2D section; switch to the 4× objective
[input 2] scan a 5×1 mm region to detect 2D cell areas
[input 3] move to the first detected region
[input 4] switch to the 20× objective, and acquire an image of a 3×3 mm field of view.
```

## 2. Biological Samples Used

2D section

## 3. Expected Results

Expected to achieve a low-to-high magnification imaging workflow for 2D slice samples: first scan a 5×1 mm area under a 4× objective and complete 2D cell region detection, then locate and move to the first detected cell region, and acquire a high-magnification image covering a 3×3 mm field of view under a 20× objective.

## 4. Execution Result and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution result: Switched to 4× and acquired a 5×1 mm brightfield region, detected 2D cell areas and moved to the first detection center, then switched to 20× and completed brightfield auto brightness and autofocus, but did not actually acquire the 3×3 mm high-magnification field-of-view image.
Failure analysis: The low-magnification scanning, detection, and movement to the first detection area process basically met the task requirements, and the 2D section does not require a Z-stack; however, in the final 20× stage, only the objective/channel settings, auto brightness, and autofocus were repeatedly executed, and the 3×3 mm acquisition positions were not configured, channels not added, and run_acquisition() not called, thus lacking the core final high-magnification 3×3 mm image acquisition output.

Overall assessment: Partial success
