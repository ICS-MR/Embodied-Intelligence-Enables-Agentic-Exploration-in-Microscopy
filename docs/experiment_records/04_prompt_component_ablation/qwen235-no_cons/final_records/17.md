## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete a 4× brightfield low-magnification scan of a 3×3 mm area of the organoid sample, identify organoids based on the scanned image, and record the target positions across all fields of view or tiles. Subsequently, under a 20× objective, DAPI, FITC, and TRITC fluorescence channel images should be acquired for each organoid at the recorded positions, with Z-stack acquisition reasonably included according to organoid thickness.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The workflow completed 4× brightfield 3×3 mm area acquisition, organoid detection, and recorded positions in JSON, then switched to 20× and configured acquisition of three-channel fluorescence images (DAPI, FITC, TRITC) for the detected organoids.
Failure analysis: The core positioning and three-channel acquisition workflow basically covered the task requirements, and automatic brightness and autofocus were performed prior to 4× brightfield acquisition. However, the sample consists of three-dimensional organoid structures. The final 20× target acquisition stage explicitly did not configure Z-stack, nor was it explained why single-plane acquisition was sufficient. As a result, the high-magnification fluorescence results may not represent the complete three-dimensional signal of the organoids, and therefore cannot be judged as fully successful. An additional minor risk is that the 20× fluorescence acquisition only focused on the current field of view before loading targets, without individually focusing on each organoid target position or compensating with Z-stack.

Overall assessment: Partial success
