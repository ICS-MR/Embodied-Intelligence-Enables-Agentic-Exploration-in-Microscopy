## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to complete low-magnification brightfield imaging of a 2×2 mm array region of the organoid sample, and from the brightfield image, obtain and load the coordinates of each organoid via organoid localization analysis. Subsequently, switch to a 20× objective, acquire images at each organoid position in the order of blue, green, and red fluorescence channels, and configure or cover the necessary Z-axis range according to organoid thickness.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution Results: The actual execution completed low-magnification 4x brightfield acquisition of a 2×2 mm area, organoid position detection, loading of coordinates, and movement to the first organoid with focusing, but did not perform the final 20x blue/green/red fluorescence sequential acquisition for each organoid.
Failure analysis: The low-magnification brightfield acquisition used automatic brightness and autofocus, and the 2×2 mm area was acquired; subsequently, organoid detection was performed on the brightfield image and organoid_locations_list.json was generated, satisfying the localization part. However, the original execution record only loaded coordinates and moved to the first organoid, without iterating through all organoids; the planned Z-stack range recommendation and 20x DAPI/FITC/TRITC or blue/green/red three-fluorescence-channel acquisition were not actually executed. Since organoids are three-dimensional targets, the final high-magnification fluorescence acquisition should also have been configured or at least considered Z-stack, which was not completed in the record. Therefore, the core task was only half completed and cannot be judged as successful.

Overall assessment: Partial success
