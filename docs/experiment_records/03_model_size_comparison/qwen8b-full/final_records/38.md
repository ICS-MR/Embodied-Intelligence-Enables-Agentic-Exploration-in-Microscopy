## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

Expected to achieve whole-plate imaging of organoids in a 96-well plate according to well coordinates, with repeated acquisition of blue and green fluorescence channels at 1-hour intervals over the set total duration, and to obtain corresponding Z-stack image sequences for each well. Ultimately, this will form a fluorescence microscopic imaging dataset of organoids that can be tracked by well, time point, channel, and Z-level.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The protocol set a 20× objective, generated 96-well coordinates, and configured the DAPI blue channel for 96 wells, Z-stack, and 24 frames per hour acquisition, then attempted to acquire the FITC green channel well by well.
Failure analysis: The core objectives were not fully met: the blue and green channels should be acquired as a time series of 96-well organoids every hour, but the records show that DAPI 24-hour acquisition was completed first, followed by FITC acquisition, leading to unsynchronized blue/green time points. Additionally, after the first run_acquisition, the acquisition queue is cleared, and time series and Z-stack were not re-configured before FITC acquisition, so the green channel was likely not repeatedly acquired every hour and also lacked the Z-stack required for organoids. There is also a risk from add_channels being called inside each well loop, repeatedly adding the same channel configuration.

Overall assessment: Partial success
