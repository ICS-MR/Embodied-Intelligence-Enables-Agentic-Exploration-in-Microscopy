It is expected to complete a 10× low-magnification brightfield scan of a 4×4 mm area of the organoid sample, identify organoid positions through image analysis, and record their coordinates. Subsequently, the first organoid coordinate should be loaded and the objective switched to 20×, acquire images in the blue and green fluorescence channels at that position, and configure or consider Z-axis acquisition for the three-dimensional organoid target.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The system successfully completed the configuration and operation of automatic brightness/focus and stitched acquisition for a 10× brightfield, 4×4 mm area, but did not actually perform organoid detection or coordinate recording. The subsequent 20× blue/green fluorescence Z-stack acquisition relied on an incorrect target position file.
Failure analysis: The 10× brightfield scanning portion basically met the requirements; however, during the image analysis stage, only error prompts were output, the organoid detection function was not called, and no organoid coordinate JSON was generated/recorded. Subsequently, brightfield_10x.ome.tif was loaded instead of the detection result JSON, making it impossible to prove that the system had moved to the “first organoid” position. Therefore, even though the code configured blue/green channels and Z-stack for 20× DAPI/FITC acquisition, there was no valid basis for target localization, and the core objective was not fully closed-loop.

Overall assessment: Partial success
