## 3. Expected Results

Bright-field/transmitted light and multiple fluorescence channel imaging of the organoid sample should be completed, including Z-stack acquisition covering the organoid thickness range. After projection, each fluorescence signal should be overlaid with the bright-field channel (Gray/Grey/brightfield) using merge_channels(). The output should reflect the organoid structure and the spatial correspondence of different fluorescence signals against the transmitted light background.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Cause Analysis

Execution results: The workflow acquired single-layer images of the current organoid field in brightfield, DAPI, FITC, and TRITC channels, and attempted to use merge_channels to synthesize an overlay image combining the brightfield and multiple fluorescence channels.
Failure analysis: The primary objectives of brightfield and multi-fluorescence acquisition and overlay were covered; automatic brightness and focus were also executed before brightfield acquisition. However, organoids are three-dimensional samples, and the records explicitly indicate that Z-stack was not configured, nor was projection applied to the Z-stack before merging. This fails to meet the task-specific criteria requiring “organoid Z-stack, and merge_channels after projection,” so the result can only be judged as partially successful. Additionally, it is necessary to verify whether the case of the Brightfield color parameter is accepted by the tool, but the main shortcoming is the lack of 3D stack acquisition and projection.

Overall assessment: Partial success
