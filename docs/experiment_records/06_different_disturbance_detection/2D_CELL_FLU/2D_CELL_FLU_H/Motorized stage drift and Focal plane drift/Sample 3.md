Relative to the initial reference state, a pronounced global displacement of the specimen towards the upper-right quadrant of the field of view was observed. Concurrently, the image exhibited marked blurring: fine structural detail was diminished, specimen boundaries were no longer sharply defined, consistent with a defocus condition. Illumination intensity was reduced compared with baseline, and the fluorescence signal was significantly attenuated and diffusely distributed, with compromised spatial uniformity. No overt contamination was detected.

Detected Anomalies

Three anomalies were identified:

Specimen translation — a global positional shift attributable to drift of the motorized stage.

Focal plane drift — manifesting as a pronounced defocus blur across the image, with loss of boundary definition.

Fluorescence anomaly — attenuated and non-uniformly distributed fluorescence signal relative to the reference state.
