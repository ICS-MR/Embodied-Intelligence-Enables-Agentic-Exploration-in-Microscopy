## 2. Biological Samples Used

Organoids

## 3. Expected Results

It is expected to achieve low-magnification, full-area localization and acquisition of organoids within a 1 mm circular gel droplet. A ~1000 × 1000 µm square field of view will cover the region and identify the coordinates of each organoid. The system will then switch to a 20× objective to sequentially acquire a status image of each organoid according to the coordinates, and at the high-magnification stage configure or appropriately consider Z‑stack acquisition to reflect the three‑dimensional structural state of the organoids.

## 4. Execution Results and Failure Analysis

Execution results: The execution record completed a 4× brightfield acquisition of an approximately 1200×1200 µm region, invoked organoid detection, and configured a 20× brightfield Z-stack multi-position acquisition.
Failure analysis: The critical issue is that the direct return values from the detection function should be treated as image pixel coordinates, but the workflow mistakenly used them directly as stage micrometer coordinates for moving and configuring the 20× acquisition positions. Moreover, the physical center coordinates in the detection JSON were not read via `load_target_locations`. As a result, the 20× sequential acquisition likely deviated from the true organoids within the 1 mm gel droplet. Therefore, although the low-magnification acquisition and detection invocation were performed, the core step of sequential 20× acquisition based on each detected organoid cannot be confirmed as completed. Other aspects—such as low-magnification area coverage, brightfield auto-brightness/autofocus, representative auto-brightness/autofocus on the first 20× target, and the consideration of high-magnification Z-stack—essentially met the requirements, but they cannot compensate for the erroneous use of target coordinates.

Overall assessment: Failure
